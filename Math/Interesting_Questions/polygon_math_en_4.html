Rectangle vs Square
inside a Regular 12-sided Polygon

Step by step — easy to follow!

📋 Question

A rectangle and a square each have their vertices on the vertices of two identical regular 12-sided polygons. The ratio of the rectangle's area to the square's area is a : b in its simplest form. Find a + b.

Rectangle
Square
💡
Set Up — Label the Radius R

Both polygons are identical. Call the distance from the centre to any corner R.

A regular 12-gon has 12 equal sides, so moving one step from corner to corner sweeps a centre angle of:

360° ÷ 12 = 30° per step
📐 Key fact (no trigonometry needed!):
In a right-angled triangle where one angle is 30°,
the side opposite the 30° angle = 12 the hypotenuse.
1
Area of the Rectangle

The short sides AB and DC are each one side of the 12-gon.

Draw two radii OA and OB from the centre O. Because A and B are 1 step apart, the angle AOB = 30°.

Drop a perpendicular from A to line OB. In the right triangle formed:

Hypotenuse = OA = R
Angle at O = 30°
∴ opposite side = 12R

This 12R is also the height from A to the long diagonal BD (= 2R).

Triangle ABD (base 2R, height 12R):

△ABD = 12 × 2R × 12R = 12R²

Rectangle = 2 × △ABD:

Rectangle = 2 × 12R² = R²
💡 The 30° is at the centre — between the two radii OA and OB — not at the corner!
2
Area of the Square

The square's corners sit at every 3rd vertex of the 12-gon (3 × 30° = 90° — perfect right angles!).

The two diagonals both equal 2R and cross at centre O at right angles, cutting the square into 4 equal right triangles.

Each triangle has two sides = R with a 90° angle between them:

1 triangle = 12 × R × R = 12R²
Square = 4 × 12R² = 2R²
💡 The square's diagonals always bisect each other at 90° — so all 4 triangles are identical!
3
Find the Ratio a : b
Rectangle = R²
Square     = 2R²

Cancel R² from both sides:

a : b = R² : 2R² = 1 : 2
✅ 1 and 2 share no common factors — already in simplest form!
🎉
Final Answer

a = 1    b = 2

a + b = 1 + 2 = 3
🌟 Key steps to remember:
1. Label the circumradius R. Any diagonal through the centre = 2R.
2. Draw radii OA and OB — they form 30° at the centre (1 step on the 12-gon).
3. The perpendicular from A to OB = 12R (30° rule: opposite = half hypotenuse).
4. Rectangle = 2 × △ABC = 2 × 12 × 2R × 12R = R².
5. Square = 4 × 12R² = 2R².   Ratio = 1 : 2.
