Parallelogram Area Problem

A step-by-step geometric proof using area ratios

Given

ABCD is a parallelogram. E is the midpoint of AD. F is the midpoint of EC.
The area of triangle BFD = 12 cm².
Find the area of parallelogram ABCD.

Diagram

The Setup

Parallelogram ABCD with E (midpoint of AD), F (midpoint of EC), and shaded triangle BFD

Strategy

Key Idea

We express every area as a fraction of S (the total area of ABCD), then use the fact that:

Area BFD = Area BCD − Area BFC − Area DFC
Core principle: When two triangles share the same apex (top vertex), their areas are proportional to their bases. We use this repeatedly to split areas in half.
Proof

Step-by-Step Solution

1

Area of △BCD = 12S

Diagonal BD splits parallelogram ABCD into two congruent triangles (△ABD and △BCD) with equal areas.

12S 12S
Area △BCD = 12S
2

Area of △BEC = 12S

△BEC and △BCD share the same base BC. Since AD ∥ BC, points E and D are the same perpendicular distance from BC — so they have the same height. Equal base, equal height → equal area.

12S
Area △BEC = 12S
Key: AD ∥ BC, so E and D are the same height above BC. Same base BC, same height → △BEC = △BCD.
3

Area of △DEC = 14S

△DEC and △BEC share the same base EC. Since E is the midpoint of AD, DE = 12BC. The height from D to EC is half the height from B to EC.

Area △DEC = 12 × Area △BEC = 14S
Why? △DEC and △BEC share same height. And DE = 12 BC.
4

Split at F (midpoint of EC)

F is the midpoint of EC, so EF = FC.

14S

△BFC = 12 × △BEC — share apex B, and FC = 12 EC (since F is midpoint of EC).

Area △BFC = 12 × 12S = 14S

△DFC = 12 × △DEC — share apex D, and FC = 12 EC.

Area △DFC = 12 × 14S = 18S
5

Calculate Area BFD

Putting It All Together

Area Summary

Area △BCD=12S
Area △BFC=14S
Area △DFC=18S
Area △BFD = 12S − 14S − 18S = 18S
12 = 18S  →  S = 96 cm²
